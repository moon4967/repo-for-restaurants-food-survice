
# Restaurant Food Ordering System - Information Gathering

## Executive Summary
This document outlines the methodology used to collect requirements-relevant information from customers and restaurant staff prior to defining the PRD and SRS, ensuring requirements are grounded in real operational pain points rather than assumptions.

## Objectives
1. Validate the problems identified in the Problem Statement with first-hand evidence.
2. Quantify how widespread key pain points are across customers.
3. Capture operational detail needed to define accurate functional requirements.

## Methods Applied
| Method ID | Method | Purpose | Participants/Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| M-01 | Interviews | In-depth understanding of operational workflow and pain points | Owner, front-of-house staff, kitchen staff, regular customers |
| M-02 | Surveys | Quantify customer preferences and frustration patterns at scale | Broader customer base |
| M-03 | Direct Observation | Validate described workflow against actual peak-hour behavior | On-site service period |
| M-04 | Document Review | Understand existing menu/billing structure | Paper menus, sample receipts |

## Methodology Justification
| Method | Justification |
|---|---|
| Interviews | Open-ended format surfaces root causes that closed questions miss |
| Surveys | Scales feedback collection across a larger customer sample |
| Observation | Confirms self-reported workflow matches actual behavior under load |
| Document Review | Establishes baseline data structure (categories, pricing format) for the system |

## Key Findings Summary
| Theme | Source | Insight |
|---|---|---|
| Order accuracy | Interviews + Survey | Primary recurring complaint across staff and customers |
| Status visibility | Interviews + Survey | Customers want real-time updates on order progress |
| Peak-hour overload | Interviews + Observation | Manual processes break down most under high order volume |
