# 09 — User Personas
## Project: Restaurant Food Ordering System

---

## What is a User Persona?
A user persona is a fictional but research-based profile representing a key type of user who will interact with the system. Personas help the team design features that truly meet user needs.

---

## Persona 1: Rahel Ahmed — The Busy Customer

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Name** | Rahel Ahmed |
| **Age** | 26 years old |
| **Occupation** | Software Developer |
| **Location** | Dhaka, Bangladesh |
| **Tech Comfort** | High — uses smartphone daily |
| **Goal** | Order food quickly without calling the restaurant |

### Background
Rahel works long hours at a tech company. He often orders lunch and dinner from nearby restaurants but finds calling frustrating — lines are busy, orders get mixed up, and he has no way to track his food. He wants a fast, reliable way to order from his phone.

### Frustrations
- Calling restaurants is slow and error-prone
- No way to see the full menu with prices and photos
- Cannot track whether his order is being prepared
- Payment is always cash — no digital option

### Goals
- Browse full menu with photos and prices
- Order in under 3 minutes
- Pay online (bKash or card)
- Know exactly when his food will be ready

### Quote
> "I just want to pick my food, pay, and know it's coming. Why is that so hard?"

## Persona 2: Fatema Begum — The Restaurant Manager

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Name** | Fatema Begum |
| **Age** | 38 years old |
| **Occupation** | Restaurant Owner / Manager |
| **Location** | Chittagong, Bangladesh |
| **Tech Comfort** | Medium — uses Facebook and WhatsApp |
| **Goal** | Manage orders efficiently and reduce staff workload |

### Background
Fatema runs a mid-sized restaurant with 8 staff members. During peak hours, her team struggles to manage phone orders, dine-in tables, and takeaway at the same time. She has heard about digital ordering systems and wants to modernize her restaurant without spending too much.

### Frustrations
- Orders coming through phone, WhatsApp, and walk-in simultaneously cause confusion
- No centralized system to track what is ordered, paid, or pending
- Manual billing causes errors and delays
- Cannot see daily sales figures easily

### Goals
- One dashboard to manage all incoming orders
- Easily add or remove menu items and update prices
- See daily and monthly sales reports
- Reduce order mistakes by kitchen staff

### Quote
> "I need to know what's happening in my restaurant without running around asking everyone."

## Persona 3: Karim — The Kitchen Staff

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Name** | Md. Karim |
| **Age** | 22 years old |
| **Occupation** | Cook / Kitchen Staff |
| **Location** | Chittagong, Bangladesh |
| **Tech Comfort** | Low — basic smartphone user |
| **Goal** | Receive clear orders without paper slips |

### Background
Karim works in the kitchen and receives handwritten order slips from waiters. Slips are often illegible or lost during busy hours. He needs a simple screen in the kitchen showing what to prepare, in what order, and any special customer notes.

### Frustrations
- Handwritten slips are hard to read
- Slips get lost or mixed up during rush hours
- No way to signal that an order is ready without shouting

### Goals
- See a clear digital list of pending orders
- View special instructions (e.g., "no onions", "extra spicy")
- Mark an order as "Ready" with one tap

### Quote
> "Just show me what to cook. I'll cook it."

## Persona 4: Nila — The First-Time Customer

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Name** | Nila Chowdhury |
| **Age** | 19 years old |
| **Occupation** | University Student |
| **Location** | Chittagong, Bangladesh |
| **Tech Comfort** | Very High — active on social media |
| **Goal** | Try new restaurants and share experience with friends |

### Background
Nila discovers restaurants through Instagram and Facebook. She wants to check the menu, see food photos, and read reviews before ordering. She often orders with friends and needs to split or combine orders easily.

### Frustrations
- Restaurant websites are outdated or don't have full menus
- No food photos to help decide what to order
- Difficult to order for a group

### Goals
- Browse menu with attractive photos
- Read and leave reviews for food items
- Share order details with friends

### Quote
> "I eat with my eyes first. If I can't see the food, I'm not ordering."
